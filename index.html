Research

I am part of the Machine Teaching and Human Intelligence Research Group at McMaster University, supervised by Dr. Swati Mishra. My interests lie in human-centered artificial intelligence, societal impacts of machine learning systems, operations research, and other relevant fields.

In related work, we developed "STORYBLOCKS" - an interactive framework that utilizes Socratic Questioning technique and combinatorial optimization to assist writers with complex idea exploration and narrative structure planning. The result of this project have been written up in a first-author paper and is under review for CHI 2026, the top conference in Human-Computer Interaction.

My current research explores how recommender systems shape online news exposure and their role in creating or mitigating filter bubbles. Complementing this, I am also investigating how user behaviors impact the system performance in a personalized news recommendation setting.

    Analyzing Recommender Systems‚Äô Effects on Filter Bubbles (Paper)

    This paper explores how recommender systems shape news exposure and whether they create ‚Äúfilter bubbles‚Äù - the phenomenon where excessive content provided by recommender systems reinforces users‚Äô pre-existing perspectives and beliefs. Using a collaborative filtering model and user studies, I found the system did not strongly reinforce bias: participants saw a mix of perspectives, and some even shifted their views.
